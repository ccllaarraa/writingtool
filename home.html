About this website
This websites aims to function as a writing tool.

It can be useful when
— Wanting to keep writing daily practice
— Facing a creative block and see what happens… magic…
— Wanting to experiment with automatic writing
— Needing to stay focused without a loud surrounding internet

How it works
— Start writing
— You won’t be able to see your text, it’s .i……n…v…….i..…s….i.…b…l……e
— Let go…
— And keep writing
— Don’t change tab, or you will lose your text!
— Click on the clock if you want a 10 minutes timer
— You can change the background colour depending on your mood

When finished
— When done and curious to read what you wrote, enter your email address and press “Send”
— Only you will receive and see your text
— After a while you will have collected a good amount of text and have a nice archive

Ideas
— Write in a calm room
— Write in a loud room
— Write in a crowd and look mysterious writing invisible words
— Find an object and write about it
— Write from the point of view of the corner of the room
— Write only using a pronoun other than “I”
— Don’t stop typing until the times clocks
— Listen to music while writing and when stuck use the lyrics to fill the void
— Write outside on a bench
— Write as fast and as loud as you can

This website is inspired by a variety of writing exercices I’ve encountered
— One from my teachers (Phil Baber and Louis Luthi) when starting to write our thesis : we were in the mountain in Switzerland and had to gather thoughts and directions with no wifi, no external ressources, only what we have in our heads, our memories.
— One from my friend Oliver Boulton who encountered it during his studies at Werplaats Typographie : write in white and don’t see what you write.
— One from Emily Segal during a workshop at the Rietveld Academie, inspired herself by the book “Writing down the Bones” by Natalie Goldberg: keep writing, don’t stop until the timer is over.
